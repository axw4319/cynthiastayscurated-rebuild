Fossil Farmstay: How We Turned a 1,200 sq ft Rental Into a Top 1% Booked Airbnb

A full case study on designing, styling, and launching a short-term rental that booked out within 48 hours of going live — with exact sources, paint colors, and budget breakdown.

Key Takeaways

  • Budget: $28,000 total furnishings & decor for 1,200 sq ft
  • Timeline: 6 weeks from concept to first booking
  • Result: First booking within 48 hours of listing; fully booked 60 days out in first week
  • ROI: Average nightly rate 38% above comparable local listings
  • Core strategy: Editorial-quality photography + intentional sourcing + guest-proof materials

When we first walked into the space that would become Fossil Farmstay, it was a blank 1,200 square foot shell with good bones and zero personality. Six weeks later, it welcomed its first guests — and within 48 hours of the listing going live, we had our first booking. Within the first week, the property was fully booked 60 days out.

This post is the full case study: every decision we made, why we made it, how much it cost, and what we'd do differently. If you're planning to design a short-term rental or refresh an existing one, bookmark this — it's everything we've learned from designing, testing, and hosting.

Location Joshua Tree, CA
Square Footage 1,200 sq ft
Bedrooms / Baths 2 BR / 2 BA
Project Type New STR Design
Budget $28,000
Timeline 6 Weeks

The Challenge

The owners had purchased the property as their first short-term rental investment. They knew they wanted something distinctive — not another beige "Airbnb-core" box — but they also needed it to be durable, photogenic, and quick to turn over between guests. Every design decision had to serve three masters: the guest experience, the photographs (which is what sells the listing), and the cleaning crew.

We also had a hard constraint: the listing needed to go live before the high season started in Joshua Tree. That meant sourcing, installing, and photographing a complete home in six weeks.

The Vision

We landed on what we called "desert library" — a warm, layered aesthetic that felt collected rather than decorated. The palette started with inky navy, warm cognac leather, and soft oat linen, with small hits of terracotta for contrast. Rather than leaning into the expected cactus-and-turquoise desert cliché, we wanted the interior to feel like a retreat from the landscape outside.

Great rental design is invisible. Guests should feel the intention without having to name it.

Pro Tip

Always budget two full sets of bedding and towels from day one. The math works out to less than one no-show booking over the first year, and it keeps your turnover timeline tight when the washer gets backed up.

The Paint Colors

Paint was the single highest-leverage decision in this project. We used three colors across the whole home:

  • Walls (main living areas): Benjamin Moore Simply White OC-117
  • Trim throughout: Benjamin Moore Wrought Iron 2124-10
  • Living room accent wall: Farrow & Ball Inchyra Blue No. 289

The Inchyra Blue accent wall is the single most photographed element in the entire house. Guests post it constantly. If you do nothing else with this case study, take the paint codes.

Lessons Learned

Six months in, these are the things I'd do differently — and the things I got right and would repeat on every project going forward.

What I'd do again

  • Editorial-quality photography before the listing goes live — non-negotiable.
  • Two full sets of linens from day one.
  • Investing in a great statement light for every room.
  • A legitimate bar cart stocked with real glassware. Guests love it.

What I'd change

  • Skip the white dining chairs. Beautiful for photos, impossible to keep clean.
  • Choose a machine-washable rug for the living room next time.
  • Install dimmers in every room from day one, not as an afterthought.

The Result

Six months after launch, Fossil Farmstay averages a 94% occupancy rate and a nightly rate 38% higher than comparable listings in the area. More importantly to me: repeat guests, unprompted five-star reviews, and a steady stream of people tagging the space on Instagram because they actually want to remember it.

The numbers matter. But the real measure of rental design is whether the space is memorable — whether guests leave with the feeling that they stayed somewhere, not just somewhere they slept. That's the bar.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much did it cost to design Fossil Farmstay?

The total design and furnishing budget for the 1,200 sq ft rental was approximately $28,000, including all furniture, art, linens, kitchenware, and decor. Labor for installation was separate.

How long did the design and installation take?

The full project took 6 weeks from initial concept to first guest. Sourcing ran in parallel with demo and prep work during weeks 1–3, and install + styling happened in week 5.

What paint colors were used?

Walls: Benjamin Moore Simply White OC-117. Trim: Benjamin Moore Wrought Iron 2124-10. Accent wall in living room: Farrow & Ball Inchyra Blue No. 289.

How quickly did the property start booking?

The listing received its first booking within 48 hours of going live on Airbnb and was fully booked for the first 60 days within the first week.
