# SEO Strategy — Cynthia Stays Curated

**Goal:** Rank on Google, Bing, and inside LLM responses (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overviews) for travel advisor + short-term rental design keywords within 6–12 months.

**Competitive reality:** A new, small site cannot rank for head terms like "disney vacation packages" or "best cruise lines." Our strategy is to **own the long tail** — specific, question-format, high-intent queries where firsthand expertise beats corporate content.

---

## 1. Positioning

The unique angle Cynthia owns that no competitor does:

> *I book your trip AND I design the places you stay in.*

This hybrid lens (travel advisor + STR designer) is the most defensible moat. Every post should reinforce it — e.g., "What travel advisors notice about bad Airbnbs," "5 design lessons from staying at 50 resorts."

---

## 2. Target Audiences

1. **Vacation planners** — families, couples, friend groups planning Disney, cruises, national parks, or Sandals trips who value a human advisor.
2. **Short-term rental hosts** — new and existing Airbnb/Vrbo owners who want their property to photograph beautifully and book more nights.
3. **AI search users** — people asking ChatGPT/Perplexity "is a travel advisor worth it" or "how do I design a short-term rental."

## 5. Publish Cadence

- **Weeks 1–6:** Quick-win posts (10 of them), 2 per week. These rank fastest and drive early momentum.
- **Weeks 7–14:** LLM-citable Q&A posts (10 more). These build topical authority and start showing up in AI answers.
- **Weeks 15–24:** Hub pillars (5 pages, ~3,500 words each) + the remaining STR design posts.
- **Ongoing:** Update older posts quarterly. AI models surface recently-modified content — half of AI citations are under 13 weeks old.

## 6. On-Page Formula (use for every post)

1. **H1** = exact target question, natural phrasing
2. **Deck** (italic subtitle) = 1–2 sentence promise
3. **Meta description** = 150–160 chars, uses target keyword once, includes a benefit
4. **Byline block** = photo, name, credentials ("Licensed travel advisor since…"), published date, updated date, read time
5. **Hero image** = original photo, alt text with location + subject
6. **Key Takeaways box** (TL;DR) = 4–6 bullets that answer the query directly. AI engines extract heavily from opening 30%.
7. **Direct answer paragraph** = first 2 sentences literally answer the H1 question
8. **Table of contents** = sticky sidebar for 1,000+ word posts
9. **H2s phrased as sub-questions**, each extractable by AI
10. **Comparison tables** for "vs" posts and listicles
11. **Info callouts** ("Pro Tip", "Watch Out") every 400–600 words
12. **Original photos** every 300–400 words (beats stock images heavily)
13. **FAQ section** at the bottom with 5–8 Q&As, marked up with FAQPage schema
14. **Author bio box** with Person schema + sameAs links
15. **Related posts** (3 cards) to adjacent content in same cluster
16. **CTA block** mid-article — "Want this for your trip? Free quote."
17. **Internal links** — 3–5 per post to other cluster content
18. **External citations** — link to 1–2 authoritative sources (NPS, Disney official, tourism boards)

## 9. E-E-A-T Signals (critical for Google + LLMs)

**Experience** — first-person trip reports with dates, photos, receipts. "I stayed 4 nights at Sandals Grande St Lucian in March 2026…"

**Expertise** — credentials stated plainly in first 100 words of every post + author box. "Licensed travel advisor since [year]" or "Designer of 20+ short-term rentals."

**Authoritativeness** — sameAs links to Instagram/Pinterest/TikTok, author bio on every post, consistent voice across all content.

**Trustworthiness** — original photography, transparent pricing ($75 planning fee after 3rd revision), office hours clearly posted, real contact email, FAQ section answering the skeptical questions first.

## 10. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Plays

Things that make AI engines cite you specifically:

1. **Top-load the answer** — first paragraph literally answers the query
2. **Use specific numbers** — "48 hours", "$28,000", "1,200 sq ft" (AI loves extractable stats)
3. **Cite primary sources inline** — official NPS data, Disney World wait times, actual resort rates
4. **Update dates visibly** — every post shows "Updated Apr 10, 2026"
5. **First-person language** — "We installed", "I tested", "After 6 months of bookings"
6. **Structured lists and tables** — AI parses these more reliably than dense prose
7. **Q&A headings** — "Is Kyoto safe at night?" ranks higher than "Safety in Kyoto"
8. **FAQPage schema** everywhere
9. **Original data** — "We tracked every chip, stain, and scuff across 12 months" is untouchable by AI-fluff competitors
10. **llms.txt file** at root summarizing the site

## 14. Realistic Expectations

- **Month 1–3:** Pages getting indexed. Few rankings yet. Impressions climbing slowly.
- **Month 4–6:** First quick-win posts reaching page 2–3 of Google. Occasional LLM citations appearing.
- **Month 7–9:** Quick-wins on page 1 for long-tail queries. Hub pages ranking for mid-tail. Consistent LLM mentions for Q&A content.

The #1 determining factor is **publishing cadence and quality consistency.** Two posts a week, written with genuine firsthand expertise, beat 10 posts a week of AI-generated fluff every time.
