Your first Disney World trip can feel overwhelming — 4 parks, dozens of hotels, and more dining options than you can count. Here is what actually matters.

## When to Go

Avoid Spring Break (March–April), Christmas week, and Fourth of July. The sweet spots are early January, late August, and the first two weeks of September when crowds are lowest and prices drop.

## Where to Stay

Staying on-property matters more than most people realize. You get Early Theme Park Entry (30 minutes before the general public), free transportation, and the ability to book dining 60 days out. Value resorts start around $120/night.

## What to Book in Advance

- Park reservations (required — book before tickets sell out for your dates)
- Dining at signature restaurants 60 days out
- Lightning Lane Multi Pass on your phone each morning at 7am

## The Single Best Tip

Arrive at rope drop for your first park of the day. The first 90 minutes at any park are the quietest of the entire day. Ride your top two priorities back-to-back before the crowds arrive.
